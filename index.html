About

I have always been interested in all aspects of technology ever since I first learned how to use the computer. As a teenager, I used to spend a lot of time after school viewing the sources of other websites and learning how to make my own. I was particularly interested in graphic design and spent a few years working on layouts designed specifically for forums. However, I grew up in a family full of accountants and consequently went to college with the intention to major in it.

After taking my first accounting course, I was quickly reconsidering my major and made the switch to computer information systems. This ended up being the right decision because I loved every single course in my major, which primarily focused on applying aspects of technology to business processes. Without a full-fledged computer science background, I was very surprised yet excited that my first job out of college was as a junior developer. I fell in love with coding and after also experiencing the functional side of software development, I decided to attend the Flatiron School to learn more about web applications and improve my skillset.

MunchyFuel

MunchyFuel is a Rails application that randomly generates a picture from a restaurant and delivery information. It is integrated with Foursquare's API to randomly select restaurants nearby based on the user's location. Users can decide to "Want" or "Don't Want" the food, or flag the picture as irrelevant. Reported irrelevant photos will not be displayed again on MunchyFuel.

View live site Github
  • Technologies: Ruby on Rails, JavaScript, jQuery, Foursquare API, Geocoder, Bootstrap

Scoutr

Scoutr is a Rails application integrated with the Yelp API that creates custom itineraries with activities for the day based on user inputs and location. Users will have the ability to sort, replace, or delete activities recommended by Scoutr. Scoutr can also search for itineraries generated by other users.

View demo Github
  • Technologies: Ruby on Rails, JavaScript, AJAX, jQuery, Yelp API, Google Maps API, Geocoder, Fuzzy Search, Bootstrap
